Type: Scratch

The Spell of Battle

Objective: The objective of the game is to go to the last level and defeat the boss. This can be achieved by touching the villain and then going to the opposite side of the map.

For this Scratch game, I needed to retrace my coding skills. Even though I have past knowledge of this language, I have not used it in many years, bringing me back to square one. With my partner, Diego van Schie, I learned many things such as how to randomize the location of my character. I also learned the value of teamwork. Even though we only had the instructions of building a multi-level project, we worked together creating a fun game. We, however, had many ideas beforehand, finally deciding to choose a game about a wizard fighting his partner, and evil rouge wizard. The main character's powers slowly progress, leading to him having a "warping power", that allowed him to teleport around the map,and a lazer that will move to the location of the mouse. From each level, the character will gain one power at a time, making each level gradually difficult. At the end, we learned many values such as coding skills, teamwork, and the power of documentation.

Course: Computer Science Principles (CSP)

Durantion: March 4, 2019 - March 21, 2019

Type: Python

Natural Waters Defense Foundation

Objective: The client wants an automated process to apply to images. They want the process to use some combination of masking, shading, or combining the images with a consistent logo or superimposed image. The client enjoys abstract art as well and might like geometric shapes incorporated in the image – drawn on, as a border, or as a mask. The client enjoys participating in the creative process and will appreciate being offered a range of options (as a parameter) for one of the image operations you perform.

The design process was fairly simply. We started the project by creating a brainstorming chart and the constraints of the project. This allowed us to be able to imagine the code in our head. After that, we created a template on how we wanted to do the images as well as what the images will look like. We were happy with our templates and started working right away. With Loic’s prior knowledge, we were able to create a strong basis for our images and what we need to work on. However, we made the code to complex that we didn’t understand a way to perfectly streamline the code. This made it a little more challenging. However, we were able to use the design process and create a good image manipulator. I also learned many concepts in Environmental Sciences. We were also able to develop on each other’s strengths. Since Loic is a senior, he has more research skills than me, causing it it be easier debug and create new ideas. Ultimately both of those actions led to positive ideas for the project but the whole process would have been more streamlined had we better communicated our intentions to each other. At the end, we learned the need of teamwork and documentation.
